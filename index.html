Hearken to me, ye who follow after rightness and justice!

Loath vassals in the midden, thy treasures heaped against
Thy purloined post unbidden, my humors grow incensed
The child of my child, yet toils in thy field
Grasping plow at home, in thy wars a shield

Swollen, my sword arm has grown
Reaping from tillage in spring I had sown
Still one stalk remains to be felled
And in its seeds my birthright is held

Vacant aspects stare callous to our mortal plight
Endless labor unrewarded picturing no end in sight
The scars upon this flesh thy lash no more can batter
Renewal waits enkindled atop thy kingdom's ashes scattered

And chansons of my forebears do ring in mine ears
Drudges to their seigneurs from first to final years
